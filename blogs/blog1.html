Introduction to CFD Analysis

person
SYS_ADMIN 2024-10-14
READ_TIME: 5 MIN

Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) is the art of replacing analytical equations governing fluid flow with numerical equivalents, allowing us to predict the performance of everything from static mixers to aerodynamic wing profiles.

The Foundation: Navier-Stokes

At the core of almost all CFD problem solving are the Navier-Stokes equations. These partial differential equations describe the motion of viscous fluid substances. They represent the conservation of momentum and mass for Newtonian fluids. Solving these analytically for complex geometries is impossible, which is where CFD comes in.

Basic Workflow

A typical CFD analysis follows a rigorous three-step process:

  1. Pre-processing: This involves creating the geometry, cleaning it up, and—most crucially—generating the mesh. A good mesh is half the battle won.
  2. Solving: Configuring the physical models, boundary conditions, and choosing the right solver (e.g., icoFoam for transient laminar).
  3. Post-processing: Analyzing the results using tools like ParaView to visualize streamlines.
info PRO_TIP

Never trust a CFD result you haven't validated against experimental data or analytical solutions when possible. Garbage in, garbage out.

Conclusion

CFD is an incredibly powerful tool that bridges the gap between theoretical fluid dynamics and practical engineering application. As we dive into specific case studies in this portfolio, we'll explore exactly how these principles are applied in OpenFOAM.
